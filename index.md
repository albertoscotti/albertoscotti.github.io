## About Me
I’m Alberto Scotti, a 36-year-old Italian currently based in Ireland, after living and working in several countries along the way.

**Sport** has been a central part of my life since childhood. Over the years, I have competed at an amateur level in **multiple disciplines**, most notably _baseball, athletics, water polo, and GAA Rounders._

My interest in sports analytics developed naturally when I began applying the tools I use in my professional work (primarily _machine learning_ with **R** and **Python**, along with _data analysis_ and _data visualisation_) to the domain I am most passionate about: sport.

This platform brings those two worlds together. It serves as both a portfolio of my work and a space to explore new ideas in sports analytics, with a focus on combining technical rigour with clear, practical insights.

## My Approach to Sport Performance Analysis

I am a passionate _sports analyst_ with a strong focus on transforming data into meaningful insights that support performance, strategy, and decision‑making in sport.

I have experience developing analytical workflows that range from exploratory analysis to predictive and explanatory models, using machine‑learning approaches to **identify patterns, forecast outcomes, and support evidence‑based conclusions**. A key aspect of my work is translating these models into **clear, interpretable insights** that can be used by practitioners, rather than treating analytics as a purely technical exercise.

Through this site, I share projects, tools, and resources that reflect this approach to sports analytics, where rigorous modelling techniques, practical applications, and visual storytelling come together to highlight trends, patterns, and competitive advantages. Indeed, I am particularly interested in _how data can bridge the gap between theory and practice, supporting coaches, athletes, and organizations in understanding performance more deeply._
