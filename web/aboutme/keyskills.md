## Skills 🧳
<details open>
  <summary><strong>Multivariate Performance Modelling Across Sports</strong></summary>
Ability to design, estimate, and interpret multivariate regression models to identify key drivers of team and player performance across different sports contexts (e.g. baseball, ice hockey, basketball, football, cricket). This includes model specification choices, comparison of competing models, and translating statistical output into sport‑relevant insights.
</details>

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Value‑Based Metrics and Advanced Performance Indicators</strong></summary>
Strong understanding of value‑based performance frameworks such as, in baseball: run expectancy, run values, and Wins Above Replacement (WAR). Ability to derive these metrics from event‑level data and use them to link individual actions to team success, competitive advantage, and economic outcomes.
</details>

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Event‑Level and Spatial Data Analysis</strong></summary>
Ability to work with high‑granularity sport data (e.g. shot logs, pitch‑by‑pitch events, ball‑in‑play locations) and extract insights through spatial representations, heatmaps, and event sequencing. This includes translating raw event data into interpretable performance patterns and tactical insights.
</details>

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Probabilistic Modelling of Match Outcomes</strong></summary>
Advanced capability in modelling categorical sport outcomes (win/draw/loss) using appropriate statistical frameworks, including logistic regression and ordered logit models. Emphasis on probability estimation, uncertainty interpretation, and avoiding common pitfalls of linear probability models.
</details>

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Wearable Sensor Data Interpretation (External Load)</strong></summary>
Understanding of external workload metrics derived from wearable technologies (e.g. GPS and accelerometry), including their assumptions and limitations. Ability to critically evaluate movement‑based load, quantify effort more accurately, and contextualise workload within training and competition demands.
</details>

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Physiological Response Analysis Using Internal Load Measures</strong></summary>
Capability to analyse internal measures of athlete response (e.g. heart rate, physiological readiness indicators) to assess how individuals tolerate training stress. Includes understanding inter‑individual variability and the strengths and limitations of physiological monitoring in applied environments.
</details>

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Integrated Load Monitoring for Performance and Injury Risk</strong></summary>
Ability to combine internal and external data streams to create a more nuanced picture of training, recovery, and overuse risk. This includes critical application of frameworks such as the acute‑to‑chronic workload ratio and informed interpretation rather than blind metric adoption.
</details>

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Applied Machine Learning Pipeline Design in Sports Contexts</strong></summary>
Strong understanding of the end‑to‑end machine learning workflow for sports analytics, including problem formulation, feature selection, model choice, validation, and performance diagnostics. Particular focus on practical challenges unique to sport data (noise, small samples, leakage).
</details>

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Interpretable and Ensemble Machine Learning Methods</strong></summary>
Hands‑on experience building and evaluating machine learning models such as Support Vector Machines, decision trees, and ensemble methods (random forests, bagging, stacking). Ability to balance predictive performance with interpretability, and to combine multiple models strategically to improve robustness.
</details>

<details open>
  <summary><strong>Analytical Data Visualisation and Performance Dashboards</strong></summary>
Ability to design and implement data visualisations and analytical dashboards that translate complex sport data into actionable insights for technical and non‑technical stakeholders. This includes selecting appropriate visual encodings for spatial, temporal, and multivariate data (e.g. heatmaps, shot charts, workload trends), integrating model outputs and uncertainty, and building dashboards that support decision‑making around performance, tactics, training load, and player monitoring rather than simple descriptive reporting.
</details>
